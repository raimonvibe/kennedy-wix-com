INTRODUCTION

A brief history of eBay

eBay, founded in 1995 by Pierre Omidyar, is a global online marketplace for trade and auctions, initially operated as Auction Web in San Jose, California and was renamed eBay in 1997. It was one of the first businesses to generate and advertise an online marketplace connecting consumers and sellers of products and services (Allie Grace Garnett, 2024).        This research explores the websites e-business models, competitive strategy,digital marketing, customer traffic data, e-CRM, social network channels, and cyber security measures.

E-business models

This model is a unique blend of three streams that are critical to the business, for example, the value stream for the business partners and the buyers, the revenue stream and logical stream (Mahadevan 2000). This paragraph highlights the E-Business models the website uses.

Brokerage Model

eBay facilitates direct transactions between sellers and buyers, allowing sellers to list products like used electronics, clothing, and rare collectibles, where the seller is an individual or business selling directly to the buyer.

Advertising model

eBay's advertising model allows sellers to pay for;Promoted Listings to boost visibility in search results and product pages, generating diverse revenue streams like top-ranking listings for used iPhones.

Affiliate model

eBay uses this model for increased traffic, sales, cost effectiveness, and targeted promotions, like eBay Partner Network, where tech bloggers earn commissions on phone purchases.

Subscription model

These subscription plans are typically aimed at sellers who want to list large quantities of products or require more advanced features. Example: A seller with a Premium Store subscription, can list up to 1,000 items per month for a flat monthly fee.

Merchant model

eBay's merchant model allows businesses to sell directly to consumers, listing items individually or in bulk with fixed prices (Buy It Now) instead of auction-based listings. Example: A seller might list a;Sony 4K TV; for a fixed price of $799 with free shipping. This item would remain at this price unless the seller changes it.

Ebay's Strength and Weakness

Strengths

Ease of navigation

eBay's website is generally easy to navigate. The homepage offers clear categories and a search bar for direct product searches.

User Interface:

While it's not necessarily the most modern in design, it is functional and serves its purpose. Some users may find the layout cluttered with ads, but overall, the search functionality and product listings are straightforward.

Weaknesses

High Fees

eBay charges high fees, including listing, final value, and PayPal processing, which can be particularly costly for small sellers.

Complex Seller Policies/User Experience

eBay's seller policies can be complex, with sellers facing penalties for mistakes. Customer service can be challenging, with delayed responses and unclear guidance. The website can also feel cluttered, making navigation difficult

The competitive and digital business strategies of eBay

eBay leverages a combination of competitive and digital business strategies, by utilizing Porter's
Generic strategy of cost leadership and

Focus on Buyer-Seller Relationship

eBay differentiates itself by fostering trust between buyers and sellers through tools like ratings, feedback systems, and buyer protection policies (Laudon; Traver, 2020).

Global Reach and Niche Products

eBay allows users to buy and sell used and niche items, including collectibles and rare goods. This gives eBay a competitive advantage in catering to specialized markets (Grewal & Levy, 2022).

Improve Logistics and Delivery Systems/Enhance Mobile Experience

eBay's auction and used goods model has seen declining returns compared to fixed-price listings and new product offerings, particularly in the;new product; and;fast delivery; segments. eBay could improve its logistics infrastructure and mobile experience to compete with Amazon's efficient network and meet consumer expectations, while investing in user-friendly features like voice search integration and seamless checkout (Huang; Benyoucef, 2013, Kantar 2020).
